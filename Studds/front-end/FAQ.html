    FAQ

    Have questions related to helmets and motorcycle accessories? Unsure of what some words or terms mean in the biking world or community? Don't worry! We have you covered. Following are some of the frequently asked questions related to motorcycling necessities and experience, two-wheeler products and riding gear.

    It is important to choose a helmet that will fit you perfectly. Here are a few steps to help you find the helmet of the right size.

    • Step 1 - Use a measuring tape or string and measure around he head - above the eyebrows /ears (area usually the helmet covers). Make a note of the measurement.
    • Step 2 - Visit our dealers store or brand outlet and ask for the preferable helmet of your size (as noted).
      • Size Head Circumference (Inches) Head Circumference (Millimeters)
      XS 21.26 540
      S 22.05 560
      M 22.44 570
      L 22.83 580
      XL 23.62 600
    • Step 3 - Try the helmet on and secure it by chin straps. The helmet should touch all parts of your and. Make sure there aren't any gaps between pads and they are touching the cheeks directly.
    • Step 4 - Move your head up/down or left/right and check if helmet doesn't move independently or slip over your cheeks.
    • Step 5 - Open the straps, pull the helmet up. If getting in and out of the helmet is not requiring too much effort you have found the right fit.
    • Step 6 - Do a 10 seconds wear-check to see if the helmet is comfortable and safe to wear for a long duration.

    Always try a helmet before buying.

    Taking good care of your helmet can extend its lifespan. The best way to do it is to keep it clean. Some of the ways to take care of your helmet are:

    1. Follow manufacturer's instructions for cleaning, as some cleaning agents have chemicals which can do more harm than good.
    2. Some motorcycle cleaning kits have helmet specific cleaners as well. Use products from reputable brands only.
    3. Store the helmet correctly and safely, preferably in shade. Prolonged exposure to the sun, extreme temperature or humidity can affect the life.
    4. Avoid it from getting knocked around or scraping against sharp materials.

    It is important to know that helmet is used for protection. So, it will keep us safe, until it is in the right form and shape.

    EPS stands for expanded polystyrene, which is a crushable foam also known as styrofoam of commonly called thermocol. It is used as helmet inner lining. A polycarbonate shell us fused with the EPS lining to make up a helmet. EPS is very light in weight and responsible for absorbing the impact. It is what protects you in the event of crash or impact on the helmet.

    Though helmets do not have an 'expiry date', it is good to change your helmet in every 3-5 years, depending on your usage, maintenance, wear and tear of the helmet. As the materials used and several components of the helmet, Parts like inner lining, belts, EPS and polycarbonate shell will have gradual decay dua to more usage or passing time.Some of the other reasons to consider for changing your helmet are:

    1. If you had a crash or impact at speed of more than 80 Kmph. The damage to the helmet would be severe, even it doesn't appear externally.
    2. If you have deep scratches on the decals; streaks that do not disappear with cleaning or rubbing.
    3. If you have unglued air vents or damaged clips, frayed and worm straps, etc.

    Apart form the above, some factors like prolonged exposure to UV rays, bad weather can also impact the life of the helmet. A well-maintained helmet that has't seen a lot of action should probably be replaced after a maximum of five years of ownership;as safety should not be compromised.
